About Pongal

Pongal is a harvest festival celebrated by the Tamil community.[1]It is a celebration to thank the Sun, Mother Nature and the various farm animals that help to contribute to a bountiful harvest.[2]Celebrated over four days, Pongal also marks the beginning of the Tamil month called Thai, which is considered an auspicious month. It usually falls on the 14th or 15th of January each year. Pongal is also the name of the dish made and eaten during this festival. It is a mixture of boiled sweet rice. It is derived from the Tamil word pongu, which means “to boil over”.
